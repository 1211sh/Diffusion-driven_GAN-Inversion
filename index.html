Abstract

We present a new multi-modal face image generation method that translates a text prompt and a visual condition into a high-fidelity and photo-realistic facial image. We employ a diffusion model as an encoder to map the multi-modal inputs into the latent space of the pre-trained 2D or 3D GANs. To establish the link between the diffusion-based encoder and pre-trained GANs, we present a mapping network and a style modulation network based on cross-attention maps that encode the features of facial attributes in a diffusion model into the latent codes and adjust their values to adapt to GANs. We further present a multi-step training strategy that reflects textual and structural representations into the generated image. With GAN inversion, we can then estimate latent codes and generate 2D or multi-view facial images. By leveraging the diffusion-based encoder with our mapping and modulation networks, our model has large capabilities to predict facial attributes and generate photo-realistic outputs. We validate our method by using pre-trained 2D and 3D GANs, and our results outperform existing methods.

Overview of our method

We use the mapping network, a diffusion-based encoder, the middle and decoder blocks of a denoising U-Net, that extracts the semantic features, intermediate features, and cross-attention maps.
